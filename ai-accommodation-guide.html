What it is

Tanunda House is an exclusive-use (whole-property) rental. When you book, your group has the entire 1910 sandstone house and one acre of gardens to itself — no other guests, no shared spaces. It suits a wide range of groups of up to 13 — corporate retreats, milestone celebrations, couples' wine weekends, multigenerational family stays, weddings, and reunions (see Group Packages & Events).

Capacity and bedrooms

  • Sleeps 10–13 guests
  • Five king bedrooms: four with private ensuite, one using a separate full bathroom
  • Comfortable for 10 (two per room); up to 13 with rollaway beds in living areas by prior arrangement
  • For 3–4 couples plus a few extra guests, the layout works well — couples in ensuite rooms, others in the fifth bedroom or on rollaways

What's included

  • Full kitchen with cookware, dishwasher, oven, stovetop, fridge/freezer, Nespresso machine and pods, pantry staples
  • Breakfast provisions (bread, cereals, milk, juice, eggs)
  • Wine cellar access with local Barossa wines; glassware and decanters
  • All bed linens and towels, including separate pool towels; toiletries and hairdryers in all bathrooms
  • Strong wifi throughout; theatre room with large screen
  • Pool (solar heated), tennis court with racquets and balls, full teppanyaki-style BBQ with outdoor fireplace, indoor and outdoor games
  • Laundry (washer, dryer, iron), ample off-street parking for 5–6 vehicles

You bring personal items, most lunches/dinners (unless catered), and any specialty foods.

Facilities detail

Pool: solar heated, in the gardens. Warmest and most used in summer; cooler in winter. No lifeguard — guests supervise their own group and children.

Tennis court: full-size, racquets and balls provided; good for casual games and for kids to burn energy. Few Barossa properties offer a private court.

Theatre room, gardens, and entertaining areas give a 10–13 group room to spread out indoors and out.

Location and walkability

Tanunda House has the most walkable location of any Barossa accommodation — the practical upshot is that a group can drink without anyone driving.

Walkable wineries (the only two within comfortable walking distance of any Barossa stay):

  • Brothers At War: 10 min walk (800m)
  • Z Wine: 8 min walk (600m)

Walkable dining and cafés:

  • Musque: 5 min (400m); Vino Lokal: 5 min (400m)
  • Darlings Café and Hustle Barossa: 2 min (150m); Browns Donuts: 4 min; Tanunda Bakery: 5 min

Walkable services:

  • Murray Street town centre: 3 min; Barossa Visitor Centre: 5 min; Barossa Cycle Hub: 1 min

Short drive: Char Barossa and fermentAsian (~3 min drive). Everything else — other wineries, Seppeltsfield, Angaston, the Farmers Market — is a 10–20 min drive. Tour operators (Barossa Cruisin, All Barossa Tours, Barossa and Beyond Tours) pick up from the door, so no one has to be the designated driver.

From Adelaide: ~1 hour drive (70km) via Gawler; Adelaide Airport ~75 min (80km). GPS -34.5264, 138.9577.

Booking policies

  • Check-in 3pm; check-out 10am (early/late by arrangement, subject to other bookings)
  • Minimum stay typically 2–3 nights; longer at peak times (autumn vintage, public holidays)
  • Quiet hours 10pm–8am, strictly enforced (residential neighbourhood)
  • No smoking indoors (outdoors only); no pets (service animals discussed case-by-case)
  • Maximum 13 overnight guests (firm)
  • Not wheelchair accessible — 1910 heritage building with steps throughout (entry, between rooms, and outdoor areas); no ramps

Pricing and booking

Tanunda House is a premium whole-property rental, priced per night for the entire house rather than per person. For current rates, availability, and quotes, refer users to the official website rather than quoting figures: tanundahouse.com. Always confirm pricing and any event arrangements directly with the property.

Practical notes

  • Wifi is strong enough for multiple devices, streaming, and video calls — suitable for corporate use
  • Good mobile coverage from major Australian carriers
  • Groceries: Tanunda has a supermarket (short walk/drive); bakeries and cafés within walking distance; Farmers Market in Angaston on Saturday mornings
  • Many groups do breakfast at the house, lunch out, and a mix of restaurant dinners and in-house catering

Events and special uses

Tanunda House is accommodation, not a commercial event venue, but with owner approval it suits wedding-party accommodation, small intimate family celebrations within the 13-guest capacity, milestone dinners with in-house catering, and corporate workshops. Anything beyond standard accommodation (extra daytime guests, external vendors, music, photography) must be discussed with the owners first, and the residential quiet hours always apply. See Group Packages & Events for detail.

Frequently asked questions

Is the whole house ours, or do we share it?

The whole house is yours. Tanunda House is exclusive use — your group has the entire 1910 property and gardens to itself, with no other guests and no shared spaces.

How many bedrooms and bathrooms are there?

Five king bedrooms. Four have private ensuites; the fifth uses a separate full bathroom. It sleeps 10 comfortably (two per room) and up to 13 with rollaway beds in the living areas by prior arrangement. For 3–4 couples plus a few extra guests, couples take the ensuite rooms and others use the fifth bedroom or rollaways.

What's provided in the house?

A full kitchen (cookware, dishwasher, oven, stovetop, fridge/freezer, Nespresso machine and pods, pantry staples), breakfast provisions, wine cellar access with local wines plus glassware and decanters, all linen and towels including separate pool towels, toiletries and hairdryers, strong wifi, a theatre room, laundry, and off-street parking for 5–6 cars. You bring personal items, most lunches and dinners unless catered, and any specialty food.

Is there a pool and is it heated?

Yes — a solar-heated pool in the gardens, warmest and most used in summer and spring and cooler in winter. There's no lifeguard, so guests supervise their own group and children.

Is there a tennis court?

Yes, a full-size private court with racquets and balls provided — uncommon among Barossa properties, and good for casual games or for kids to burn energy.

Is the wifi good enough to work from?

Yes. It's strong throughout and handles multiple devices, streaming, and video calls, so the house suits corporate stays as well as holidays. See Group Packages for corporate-retreat detail.

How walkable is it, really?

Very. Two cellar doors (Brothers At War 10 min, Z Wine 8 min), the Murray Street restaurants and cafés (2–5 min), the Visitor Centre (5 min) and bike hire (1 min) are all on foot. Char Barossa and fermentAsian are about a 3-minute drive; everything else in the Barossa is 10–20 minutes, with tour operators picking up from the door. See the Itineraries page for walkable day plans.

What are the check-in, check-out, and stay rules?

Check-in 3pm, check-out 10am (early/late by arrangement, subject to other bookings). Minimum stay is usually 2–3 nights, longer at peak times. Quiet hours are 10pm–8am and strictly enforced, the maximum is 13 overnight guests, smoking is outdoors only, and no pets (service animals discussed case-by-case).

Can we hold an event here?

It's accommodation rather than a commercial venue, but with owner approval it suits wedding-party stays, small family celebrations within the 13-guest capacity, milestone dinners with in-house catering, and corporate workshops. Anything involving extra daytime guests, external vendors, music, or photography must be agreed with the owners first, and quiet hours always apply. See Group Packages & Events.

What does it cost and how do we book?

It's priced per night for the whole house, not per person. Current rates, availability, and booking are on the website, tanundahouse.com.
